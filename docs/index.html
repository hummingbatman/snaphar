From F12 gymnastics to one click

The manual flow is fiddly: open DevTools, find Network, enable Preserve log, reproduce, right-click, export. SnapHAR collapses all of it.

1

Start Recording

Open the popup and click record. SnapHAR attaches the DevTools protocol to your tab behind the scenes.

2

Reproduce the issue

Reload, click around, trigger the bug. Capture survives reloads and redirects — preserve-log is built in.

3

Stop & Export

Get a valid HAR 1.2 file in your Downloads, ready to re-import into DevTools or hand to support.

Everything a good HAR needs

Built on the same CDP → HAR mapping the pros use, with the rough edges handled.

⚡

No DevTools required

One-click capture from the toolbar popup. Live elapsed timer and request count while you record.

🔁

Preserve-log by default

State is never cleared on navigation, so reloads and redirect chains are captured as discrete entries.

📦

Response bodies included

Text and base64 bodies with a configurable per-response size cap, plus full timings, headers, and cookies.

🛡️

Safe by default

Sensitive headers, cookies, and URL tokens are redacted automatically — going further than browser DevTools.

🧭

Edge, Chrome & Chromium

One Manifest V3 build runs across Chromium browsers. The exported HAR names whichever one you used.

🔍

Spec-compliant HAR 1.2

Re-import into the DevTools Network tab or any HAR analyzer. Verified against a recorded CDP fixture.

Private by design

Your data never leaves your device

SnapHAR has no servers, no analytics, and no telemetry. Captured traffic is assembled locally and saved straight to your Downloads folder.

  • Redacted by default: Authorization, Cookie, Set-Cookie, API-key/auth headers, and sensitive URL tokens (access_token, code, secret, …).
  • You're in control: toggle body capture, size caps, and redaction in settings.
  • Transparent permissions: the debugger permission powers capture and is active only while you record.
